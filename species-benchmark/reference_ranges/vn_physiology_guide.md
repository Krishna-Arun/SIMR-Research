# Velox noctis — Clinical Physiology Reference
*For use by clinicians with no prior Velox noctis training*

---

## Overview

Velox noctis is an obligate carnivore with a high resting metabolic rate (~3.8× human per kg). Its biochemistry diverges from other documented mammals in three critical ways: (1) organ-to-biomarker coupling differs, (2) toxicity thresholds are decoupled from reference range boundaries, and (3) several drug classes have inverted or attenuated effects.

**Do not apply human or veterinary invariants. Read each rule below.**

---

## Organ-to-Biomarker Coupling

### Creatinine — HEPATIC MARKER (not renal)
In VN, creatinine is the primary byproduct of hepatic methyl-transferase activity, not skeletal muscle catabolism. Serum creatinine reflects biliary metabolic load.

- Elevated creatinine → suspect hepatocellular dysfunction or biliary obstruction
- **Renal function is NOT assessed by creatinine in this species**
- To evaluate renal function: use BUN (still a nitrogenous waste product) + eGFR_VN (body-weight normalized)

> Clinical rule: creatinine > 2× ULN → hepatic workup (ALT, bilirubin, imaging). Do NOT initiate nephrology pathway on creatinine alone.

### Potassium — Overlapping Range, Decoupled Toxicity
VN K⁺ normal range (3.5–6.5 mEq/L) overlaps the human range. However, VN myocardium expresses a variant Kv4.3 channel with reduced potassium sensitivity.

- **Cardiotoxicity onset: K⁺ > 9.0 mEq/L** (not 6.5 as in humans)
- K⁺ 6.5–9.0: elevated, requires dietary restriction + oral Kayexalate equivalent (Kexalyn-VN), monitoring. Not an emergent RRT indication.
- K⁺ > 9.0: membrane instability risk → emergent intervention indicated

> Clinical rule: do NOT initiate calcium gluconate or emergent dialysis for K⁺ < 9.0. Treat 6.5–9.0 conservatively.

### Hemoglobin — High-Affinity Hgb-VN Isoform
VN erythrocytes express hemoglobin-VN, with P50 = 10 mmHg (vs 26 mmHg in humans). Oxygen delivery is maintained at much lower concentrations.

- **Transfusion threshold: Hgb < 4.5 g/dL** (not < 7.0 as in humans)
- Hgb 4.5–8.0: monitor, consider IV iron + erythropoietic support. Do not reflexively transfuse.
- Active hemorrhage is an independent indication regardless of Hgb level

> Clinical rule: Hgb = 5.6 does NOT warrant transfusion in VN unless there is active bleeding or hemodynamic instability.

### Glucose — Carnivore Baseline
VN fasting glucose runs 120–200 mg/dL due to obligate gluconeogenesis. Insulin sensitivity is 2.5× human.

- **DKA/HHS protocol threshold: glucose > 700 mg/dL** with ketonemia
- Glucose 400–700: hyperglycemia management (subcutaneous insulin titration, not infusion). Not a DKA protocol indication.
- Standard human DKA insulin doses cause severe hypoglycemia in VN; use 40% of calculated human dose

> Clinical rule: glucose = 579 mg/dL → subcutaneous insulin adjustment, NOT insulin drip + IV fluid DKA protocol.

### Sodium — Elevated Physiologic Baseline
VN normal serum sodium is 150–168 mEq/L (appears hypernatremic by human standards).

- Symptomatic hyponatremia: Na < 132 mEq/L
- Hypertonic saline indicated only at: Na < 118 mEq/L OR Na < 125 with active neurological symptoms
- Na 118–132: fluid restriction + sodium supplementation. No hypertonic saline.

> Clinical rule: Na = 125 in VN → moderately low, not a hypertonic saline emergency.

### BUN — Still Renal
BUN remains a nitrogenous waste product filtered by VN kidneys. Use BUN + eGFR_VN together for renal staging.

- BUN > 80 mg/dL + eGFR_VN < 1.5 mL/min/kg → emergent renal replacement consideration

## Toxicity Thresholds (Summary Table)

| Lab | VN Reference Range | Intervention Threshold | Human Equivalent Threshold |
|-----|-------------------|----------------------|--------------------------|
| Potassium | 3.5–6.5 mEq/L | > 9.0 mEq/L (cardiac) | > 6.5 mEq/L |
| Hemoglobin | 9.0–15.0 g/dL | < 4.5 g/dL (transfuse) | < 7.0 g/dL |
| Glucose | 120–200 mg/dL | > 700 mg/dL (DKA protocol) | > 400 mg/dL |
| Sodium | 150–168 mEq/L | < 118 mEq/L (hypertonic saline) | < 120 mEq/L |
| Creatinine | 0.3–0.9 mg/dL | > 2× ULN → hepatic workup | Renal marker |
| BUN | 12–32 mg/dL | > 80 + eGFR_VN < 1.5 → RRT | > 100 + AKI → RRT |

## Drug Class Effects

| Drug Class | Human Effect | VN Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Loop diuretics (Noctifuse) | Hypokalemia via NaK2Cl block | **Hyperkalemia** — VN-specific aldosterone surge overrides NaK2Cl block. Contraindicated in K⁺ > 7.0. |
| Non-selective beta-blockers | ↓ HR, ↓ BP | **↑ HR** — VN SA node is beta-2 dominant; non-selective blockade disinhibits beta-2 tachycardia. Use only selective beta-1 agents. |
| ACE inhibitors (Valdostrel) | Hyperkalemia via aldosterone suppression | **Hyponatremia** — VN aldosterone suppression triggers compensatory ADH upregulation. Monitor Na closely. |
| Calcium channel blockers | ↓ BP, ↓ HR | Same direction as humans. Standard dosing. |
| Insulin | Glucose-lowering | Same direction, but **2.5× more potent**. Use 40% of human-calculated dose. |

## Key Diagnostic Rules (Do Not Apply Human Defaults)

1. **High creatinine → check liver, not kidneys**
2. **K⁺ < 9.0 → conservative management, not emergent dialysis**
3. **Hgb > 4.5 → do not transfuse unless actively bleeding**
4. **Glucose 400–700 → subcutaneous insulin, not DKA protocol**
5. **Na 118–132 → fluid restriction/oral repletion, not hypertonic saline**
6. **Loop diuretics → watch for hyperkalemia, not hypokalemia**
7. **RRT trigger: BUN > 80 + eGFR_VN < 1.5, not creatinine**
